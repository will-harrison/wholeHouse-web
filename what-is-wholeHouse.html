One app to rule them all.

Why do we have so many apps? Why do we have so many ways to send someone a message? Why are there so many unintuitive steps to view my kids' calendars?

wholeHouse combines user information, notifications, location, time, and lists - and connects them all in an seamless, intuitive experience.

We're building an app that connects your (private) data together. That means wholeHouse can make sense of your life.

One platform for all devices.

My phone doesn't know anything about my tablet, that doesn't know anything about my desktop computer, that doesn't know anything about my work computer, my TV, my car, my ...

wholeHouse is a central server that all of your devices view in real time. Now, your phone, table, computer, and eventually TV, car, alarm clock... are in sync - all the time, without your intervention.

It lives on your own private cloud.

We can host a very inexpensive, private, secure virual machine - if you have no idea what I just said.

Or you can host it yourself.

I'm developing on a raspberry pi on my shelf.

The point is that your data is yours. I don't want it, I don't want to sell it.

Open source, with an app store built in.

That means people who care about the project can contribute new and useful ways to interact with wH.

That means we can focus on building a solid core service while people dream up ways to use wH that we could never think of.
